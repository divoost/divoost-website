Complete ICP Filing Guide for Korean Companies

Without ICP filing (互联网内容提供商备案), your website cannot be hosted on Chinese servers — meaning load times of 8-15 seconds for Chinese users. Most Korean companies fail their first 2 applications. Here's the 30-day roadmap that works.

ICP vs ICP License — Know the Difference

  • ICP Filing (ICP备案): Free, required for ALL websites hosted in China (informational sites)
  • ICP License (ICP许可证): Paid (¥10k+), required for commercial websites (e-commerce, paid content)
For Tmall Global / JD Worldwide selling, you don't need ICP — those platforms handle it. For your own brand website (品牌官网), you need ICP Filing minimum.

Documents Required (Korean Company)

  1. Chinese business license — must establish WFOE or JV in China (사업자등록증 X, only Chinese entity accepted)
  2. Legal representative's Chinese ID (身份证) — must be Chinese national OR foreigner with valid work permit
  3. Domain certificate (域名证书) — domain registered in the Chinese entity's name (.cn or .com OK)
  4. Office lease (办公室租赁合同) — 12+ months remaining, Chinese address required
  5. Chinese hosting provider (Alibaba Cloud / Tencent Cloud) — ICP filing tied to provider
  6. Background verification photo — taken at provider's official backdrop (幕布)

30-Day Approval Roadmap

DAY 1–5 Pre-requisites Setup

If WFOE not yet established, this is a separate 60-90 day process. Assuming WFOE exists: purchase hosting (Alibaba Cloud ECS recommended, ~¥3,000/yr), register domain through Chinese registrar.

DAY 6–10 Document Preparation

Get business license original, legal rep ID, lease contract. Take background verification photo at provider office (Alibaba has booths in Hangzhou, Beijing, Shenzhen) OR order their backdrop kit (¥0, shipped free).

DAY 11–14 Submit to Provider

Upload documents to Alibaba Cloud ICP system. Provider reviews in 1-3 business days. Common rejections: blurry ID scan, lease address mismatch, domain owner mismatch.

DAY 15–25 MIIT Government Review

After provider approval, application goes to MIIT (工信部). Provincial review takes 7-20 business days. Beijing/Shanghai/Guangzhou are fastest (7-10 days). Tier-2 cities can take 15-20 days.

DAY 26–30 ICP Number Issued

Receive ICP number (e.g., 京ICP备20260001号). Display in website footer within 30 days. Failure to display = ¥10,000+ fine and forced site shutdown.

Top 7 Reasons for Rejection

  1. Domain registrant name ≠ business license name (must be exact match)
  2. Background photo not taken with provider's official backdrop
  3. Legal representative photo blurry or angle wrong
  4. Website content includes restricted topics (news, gaming, social — need separate license)
  5. Phone number unreachable during MIIT verification call
  6. Lease address ≠ business license address
  7. Hosting provider mismatch (filed with Alibaba but using Tencent server)

What If You Don't Have a Chinese Entity?

Three legitimate workarounds:

  • Hong Kong hosting — No ICP needed but 300-500ms latency to Beijing/Shanghai
  • CDN-only approach — Cloudflare China Network / Alibaba CDN with origin abroad
  • WeChat Mini Program instead — No ICP needed, WeChat hosts everything (best for B2C)

Cost Breakdown

  • WFOE setup (one-time): ¥30,000–80,000
  • Alibaba Cloud ECS hosting (annual): ¥3,000–8,000
  • Domain registration (annual): ¥55–200
  • ICP filing fee: ¥0 (free)
  • Third-party agency (optional): ¥3,000–8,000
Pro tip — Filing under an existing Chinese partner (distributor, JV partner) is faster (15 days) but means losing control of your domain. Long-term we always recommend your own WFOE.
