100 Forbidden Phrases in China Ad Law — 2026

China's Advertising Law (《广告法》) has the harshest penalties in the world. Fines start at 200,000 RMB ($28k) and reach 2M RMB ($280k) for repeat violations. Most Korean brands get fined within their first 3 months — not for obvious words, but for common phrases that seem harmless in English.

The Absolute Banned Words (绝对化用语)

Article 9 of the Advertising Law bans superlative/absolute claims. These trigger automatic fines:

Hidden Trap — Medical/Health Claims (医疗用语)

Cosmetics, food, and supplements cannot make any medical claims. The list of forbidden words is shockingly broad:

  • Treat / Cure (治疗 / 治愈)
  • Anti-inflammatory (消炎)
  • Anti-allergy (抗过敏)
  • Detox (排毒)
  • Anti-aging (抗衰老 — yes, even this!)
  • Whitening (美白 — restricted to NMPA-certified products only)

Safe Alternatives

  • Skin-friendly (亲肤)
  • Smoothing (柔嫩)
  • Brightening (提亮 — OK for non-medical brightening)
  • Hydrating (保湿)

Price-Related Bans

Don't claim 'lowest price' (最低价), 'cheapest' (最便宜), or 'unbeatable' (无敌). These are absolute claims under Article 9. Use 'special price' (特价) or 'promotion price' (促销价) instead.

PIPL Compliance (Personal Information Protection Law)

Since 2021, China's PIPL is similar to GDPR but stricter. For e-commerce campaigns:

  • Explicit consent required for retargeting
  • Cross-border data transfer needs CAC filing
  • Privacy policy must be in Simplified Chinese
  • Children under 14: separate consent + storage segregation

2026 Updates — What's New

  1. Livestream hosts personally liable for product claims (host fined ¥500k in 2026 March case)
  2. AI-generated images must be labeled '由AI生成' in lower-right corner
  3. Before/after photos require 7-day minimum gap disclosure
  4. Cross-border health supplements need 'imported food' label, not 'health food'

Pre-Launch Checklist

  • ✅ All copy reviewed by NMPA-registered local agency
  • ✅ No absolute words (国家级/最/第一/唯一/100%)
  • ✅ No medical claims (治疗/消炎/排毒/抗衰老)
  • ✅ Price claims have evidence (date range, source)
  • ✅ Comparison data cites third-party reports
  • ✅ AI-generated visuals labeled
  • ✅ PIPL-compliant Chinese privacy policy
